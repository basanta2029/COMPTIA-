4.0 Identity and Access Management

In today's interconnected world, effectively managing user identities and regulating access to resources is fundamental to protecting organizational assets and maintaining compliance. As you progress through this module, you'll explore the essential concepts, technologies, and best practices that organizations use to confirm user identities, control access, and safeguard sensitive information. Whether you're supporting everyday users or administering critical systems, understanding these principles is key to enabling secure, efficient business operations.

Throughout this journey, you'll engage with real-world scenarios and practical exercises designed to strengthen your ability to implement access controls across various environments. You'll gain insight into both foundational and advanced topics, from traditional models to modern strategies that respond to today's dynamic threat landscape. Regardless of your current experience level, this module will empower you to make informed decisions and contribute confidently to your organization's security posture.
In this module, you will:
Compare and contrast different access control models and explain their use cases.
Identify techniques and technologies for authentication, including methods for strengthening user credential security.
Explain authorization practices, including how permissions and policies govern access to resources.
Describe how directory services such as Active Directory and Linux-based systems manage users and groups.
Assess methods for enabling secure remote access and network authentication in distributed environments.
